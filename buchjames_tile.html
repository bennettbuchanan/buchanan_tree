Notes

"Dad only went to the 3rd grade in school. Had typhoid fever and doctor told parents to keep him out of school. Sold newspapers on streets of D.C. Seems he told me he was in Union Station [Union Station built 1907; probably the old Pennsylvania Railroad station located on the site of the present day National Gallery of Art] when President Garfield was asassinated.

Uncle Henry Rogers left him and his sisters money ($5,000 ea.?) (or $5,000 total?). He and Forestine (Aunt Floss) left home to keep money--went to Mount Vernon, N.Y. to bet on the horses. Apparently money went fast. Became tile setter, moved to Philadelphia, worked for company that made wooden fireplace mantels, etc.

Dad started tile business (partnership) with ___Sloan. Partnership dissolved unhappily. Can't remember whether business was after he worked for Phila Navy Yard or before. Left Navy Yard after six years of good service--very cold and damp work. Good money on outside ($20 / day)--(workers wearing silk shirts). All I remember was a tough struggle to get enough money to pay rent and food from early childhood, seems like, plus marriage problems with Mother Mae." [2]
