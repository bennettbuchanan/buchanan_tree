Notes:

During the Civil War, William Jefferson Buchanan served with his brother John Rowan Buchanan in the First Maryland Artillery (CSA) also known as Dement's Battery and participated in that unit's action at Gettysburg. Unit was present at Antietam but did not go into action until late in the day.

Graduated from Princeton University. Received "honors" as member of the American Whig Society there.

"Wm. J. Buchanan, a writer, had employed his pen in an effort to get Maryland to secede from the Union: Charles Branch Clark, "Politics in Maryland During the Civil War," Maryland Historical Magazine, XXXVI (Sept. 1941), pp. 242, 258, 260, 262.

Author of the following political and pro-secessionist tracts:

Maryland's Crisis: A Political Outline. By W. Jefferson Buchanan.... J.W. Randolph, Richmond, Va. 1963. 8 vo. pp. 16

Maryland's Hope: Her Trials and Interests in Connexion with the War. by W. Jefferson Buchanan. ... West & Johnston, Richmond,. 1864. 8vo, pp. 62
