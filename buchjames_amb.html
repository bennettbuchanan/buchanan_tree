Miscellaneous notes:

Inscription in Ophelia (1844): "To Mrs. James M. Buchanan from her Brother John Rowan."

Reminiscence by James M. Buchanan (d 1956). "Here are a few facts as I remember my father telling me when a boy about my grand father. He lived in the State of Md all his life and was a very prominent Lawyer and Judge in Balto. in the years of the Sixtys. He also was Postmaster of said City and also introduced the famous Buchana postage stamp that stamp collectors love to get hold of. He was United States Minister to Denmark under President Buchanan's Administration, also one of the donators to the Washington Monument in Balto. and has his name on the bronze plate on said Monument. His picture hangs in the State Department at Wash- among onther foreign Ministers. He died around 1880 and is buried in Greenmount Cemetary, Balto Md. I have heard tell that some of his ancestors were buried in Druid Hill park, Balto Md. He was the father of 5 Sons and 1 Daughter.-- John, James, Buck, Harney, Francis-Key and Rebecca. They are all deceased. There remains only my 4 Sisters and self and my 4 Children. P.S. My Grand father was related to the well known Perrine family of Balto. also." {Buchanan to Arthur Howell, < Sept. 1, 1929, responding to Howell to Buchanan on 9.1.1929 requesting family history for genealogy of Howell's family and particularly that of John and Ann Howell "because it is believed by some of the descendants that he was heir to title and large estate, also considerable money, which is held in chancery by British Government for want of legal proof of heirs."}
