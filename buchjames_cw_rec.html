National Archives Military Records for James M. Buchanan, Third Maryland Artillery

Enlisted in 3rd Battery Maryland Artillery by Captain Henry B. Latrobe in Richmond Va. "for the duration" as a "3 Sergt."

January 14 to May 1, 1862

Was marked as "present" and "have not been paid" as a 2 Sergt. in the Third Battery Maryland Artillery.

March 7, 1862

"Captain Latrobe's artillery company, with two howitzers, marched to the gap from Knoxville" (Cumberland Gap) according to Col. D. Leadbetter in a letter to General S. Cooper.

March 15, 1862

Major General E. Kirby Smith writes "I have orderd forward to Clinton two Alabama regiments, The Third Regiment Tennessee Volunteers, a batallion of North Carolina Volunteers, a section (two pieces) Third Maryland Artillery...the whole under the command of Col. D. Leadbetter." [Clinton is 34 miles northwest of Knoxville].

May and June 1862

Was "present" as 1 Sergt in the Third Battery Maryland Artillery. Still "have not been paid."

May to November 1862

Was present and paid on May 1, 1862 by Maj. A.E. Jackson as a "pvt"

September 1, 1862

"Sergt. J. M. Buchanan reduced to the ranks by order of Genl. Reynolds."

September 1, 1862

Enlisted by Capt. Rowan in Knoxville as a private in Capt. Wm. H. Rowan's Company of Kentucky Partisan Ranges, and listed as present on their must roll from Sept. 1, 1862 to Feb. 28, 1863. [two attached notes] "For use of horse...72.00" and "transferred from Latrobe Battery by order of Genl. Heth."

Feb. 28, 1863

Paid by Capt. E. L. Herd

Feb. 28 to June 30, 1863

Company muster role of Co. C., Jessee's Batallion, Kentucky Independent Rangers shows "Pvt J Buchanan" present as having enlisted Nov. 14, 1862 in Richmond by Lt. Rowan for "2 yrs or war" and "40 cts per day for hire and risk of horse $48/100."

March and April 1863

Marked as "absent" from (1st) Co. K., 6 Reg't Georgia Cav. remarks on this muster include "transferred from Latrobe's Battery" and "absent without leave."

October 11, 1863

In a report of a retreat which ended in Muscle Shoals in Northwest Alabama, a Col. George Hodge reports that "Major Tenney, Captain Rowan, Major McConnel were conspicuous in leading their battalions."

No further records
