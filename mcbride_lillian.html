Vitals:

  • Born: August 9, 1883 {passport application of JMBuchanan d 1976}
  • Place of birth: Kansas City {"on a farm on Old Shawnee road...[1]} Elk County {passport application of JMBuchanan d 1976}Note the county seat of Elk County is Howard which is named after Oliver O. Howard. Howard was the Union General in charge of the Philadelphia Brigade at Antietam. Union policy after the war was to provide Union soldiers land grants in Kansas which is the probable reason the McBride family went there for a short time.

Notes

[Elizabeth Buchanan Lipp] "My mother...Lillian McBride was very beautiful as I had heard people tell me all my life that knew her--she was a librarian in Phila. -- getting the job at 16 after passing the exam with the highest mark--making one mistake--she left the "s" off of Les Miserables--but she had read all of her life everything that was ever written and could quote Shakespeare's plays "backwards and forwards" as they used to say. She quoted Robert Louis Stevenson to us and any fairy tales we wanted. She did not need the books. She had a very vivacious personality and people would say to me--"there was no-one like your mother." she worked until she was 24--supporting her mother and her sister Gladys. She won a complete scholarship to the Academy of Fine Arts and her mother wouldn't let her take it--as she said--"that is only for rich men's daughters." She was accepted for a nursing course but got the same answer from her mother." [1]

Recipient "through the personal representative of Lillian McBride Buchanan" of part of estate of Francis M. McBride (Orphans Court of Philadelphia County, April Term, 1885) through Elizabeth McBride. Will confirmed June 27, 1921.
