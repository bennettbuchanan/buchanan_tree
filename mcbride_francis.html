Civil War Service:

"Joined for duty and enrolled, August 10, 1861 at Philadelphia" for a period of 3 years; 72nd Pa. Co F. First Lt. / "Promoted July 1, 1862 from First Lt. of Co. F vice Chas McGonigle killed at Save Station, June 29th." / Resigned March 27, 1863 after being wounded at Fredericksburg, VA. {Source: RG 94 Records of the Adjutant General's Office, 1789-1917, Civil War (Union) Compiled Military, 72nd Pennsylvania Infantry, Box 35035 Martin, G. to McGrittigan.}

"This is to certify that I have carefully examined Capt. Frank McBride, Co. B, 72nd Pa. Voal. and find that he is and has for a long time been suffering from Chronic Diarrhaea, and from lameness arising from a Gunshot wound received in action at Fredericksburg; and furthermore it is my opinion that he will not be able to do military duty or endure the hardships of the field without permanent disability or loss of life." [Charles C. Bombauch, Act Surgeon, U.S.A. General Hospital, Chestnut Hill, March 10, 1863 in RG 94 Records of the Adjutant General's Office, 1789-1917, Civil War (Union) Compiled Military, 72nd Pennsylvania Infantry, Box 35035 Martin, G. to McGrittigan.
