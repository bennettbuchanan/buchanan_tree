Reid Family

The Reid family appears throughout Buchanan correspondence and quite a few images of members of the family are in the collection.

According to a penciled note on the verso of a photograph of the Reid family and written by James Buchanan (tile), "Mrs. Reid was the daughter of Mr. and Mrs. John Rowan of Bardstown, Ky." He identified the three young men standing behind Mrs. Reid as "My Cousins."

James Buchanan (tile) seems to have maintained a life-long correspondence with his cousin John Rowan Reid.

From San Diego Union, 3/18/1934

"in 1848 John Rowan, Jr., U.S. minister to Italy...Rowan was the grandfather of San Diegan John Rowan Reid. Judge John Rowan, great grandfather of Reid figured in early Kentucky history."

John Rowan Reid's father was Col. Samuel Chester Reid. SCR had five children mentioned in his obituary appearing in the Washington Post on February 24, 1941. They are:

  • John Rowan Reid (of Philadelphia)
  • Mrs. K. K. Kennedy (of Philadelphia)
  • Mrs. F. H. Parkhurst (of Bangor, ME)
  • Mr. S. Chester Reid (of New York)
  • Mr. W. S. Reid ( no location)

The obituary continues: "in 1866 Col. Reid, then a resident of Kentucky, married Miss Josie Rowan, daughter of the United States Minister to Naples and graddaughter of the celebrated statesman and Senator from Kentucky, John Rowan."

A note scribbled at the bottom of the obituary by James M. Buchanan (tile) states: "This was the Husband of my Cousin, Mrs. J. Reid, formerly Miss Rowan."

From a letter from Betty Buchanan to Lillian Buchanan: "Capt. William H. Rowand and James M. B., Jr. [cw] were first cousins--Capt. Rowan--son of Judge Rowan (their mothers were sisters). Rowan Buchanan was named after Judge Rowan."
