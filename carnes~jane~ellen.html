The following is a transcription of an obituary that appeared in an unknown newspaper in 1901. The article is pasted to cardboard backing. A handwritten note by James M. Buchanan (d 1956) to his son James M. Buchanan (d 1976) reads:

"Jim,

This is, or was, your great Grand Mother. Keep it for Reference. Dad Buchanan"

Mrs. Ellen Buchanan

The body of Mrs. Ellen Buchanan, the widow of the Hon. James M. Buchanan of Baltimore, who died in Washington, was brought to Baltimore and interred beside that of her husband in the family lot in Greenmount yesterday. Prior to the removal of the body to Baltimore, which was made under the direction of Mr. E. Glenn Perine of this city, a nephew of the deceased, requiem mass was celebrated at St. Patrick's Church, Washington, by the Rev. Father Stafford. Mrs. Buchanan's husband was a cousin and namesake of President James M. Buchanan. He was a well-known lawyer in this city before the Civil War, and was Minister to Denmark during Buchanan's Administration. As Postmaster of Baltimore, he was the first to append his signature to the original issue of United States postage stamps, and samples of this issue bearing his signature are now valued at $4000 each. While in Copenhagen with her husband, Mrs. Buchanan formed the acquaintance of the present Queen of England, who is a daughter of Frederick the XII, of Denmark, the then reigning sovereign. On the accession of the Queen to the throne of England recently, Mrs. Buchanan wrote her a letter of congratulation, which is regarded as a remarkable production to emanate from a woman 90 years of age. Mrs. Buchanan was a cousin of Francis Scott Key, the author of the "Star-Spangled Banner," and it was her son, W. Jefferson Buchanan, who was the official representative of the Confederacy at the Vatican."

Newspaper account of Ellen "Karnes" Buchanan's death, Washington, D.C. 1901.
